## Section 1 — Specialty Manufacturing updates (substantive)

Cygnus's primary Track shifts from TRACK-003 to TRACK-008 SBA 504. This requires SBA 504-aware language across all four objective phases for specialty_manufacturing Member-Type.

### 1.1 Discover — Specialty Manufacturing (replaces v2 cell)

What to focus on during the Discover phase with a specialty manufacturing Member:

- **Map the decision-process structure first.** Specialty manufacturing financing decisions involve board approval, CFO/controller analysis, founder authority, and often external advisors. Surface the structure explicitly: "Who on the team needs to be part of this conversation? What's the typical board cadence for capital decisions?"
- **Confirm owner-occupancy intent early for facility decisions.** Specialty manufacturing Members acquiring real estate need to articulate whether the facility serves their own operations (SBA 504 fit) or is investment-positioned. The distinction shapes which financing structure applies and changes the underwriting path. Capture as factor early.
- **Anchor on customer-growth commitments.** Specialty manufacturing capacity decisions are typically driven by anchor-customer volume signals (preferred-supplier announcements, multi-year volume commitments, capacity-reservation conversations). Capture these as Triggers with customer_growth_announcement tag.
- **Listen for the floor-space framing.** Specialty manufacturing Members articulate physical capacity through square-footage, production-line count, or specific equipment-housing constraints. Capture verbatim — the framing matters for facility-acquisition conversations.
- **Probe regulatory-compliance context.** Specialty manufacturing businesses face industry-specific regulatory requirements (FDA, EPA, OSHA, sector-specific) that shape facility decisions. Surface regulatory context early; it constrains expansion options and shapes property selection.
- **Confirm prior banking-relationship history.** Specialty manufacturing Members often have history with multiple institutions. Past lending events shape relationship-led pitch framing.

### 1.2 Measure — Specialty Manufacturing (replaces v2 cell)

What to focus on during the Measure phase with a specialty manufacturing Member:

- **Confirm employment count band precisely for SBA 504 fit.** SBA 504 eligibility includes employee-count thresholds; capture the specific number rather than rough estimate. Specialty manufacturing Members typically know their headcount precisely.
- **Quantify capacity utilization with production-level precision.** Specialty manufacturing Members track capacity in production-line hours, machine utilization %, or output-units per shift. Capture the specific measure they use, not a generic %.
- **Quantify customer-growth volume commitments.** Anchor customers' volume signals translate to specific capacity demand. "If [customer] hits their stated growth, what does that require from your operations?" Member typically has this number; capture it.
- **Capture annual revenue band carefully.** Specialty manufacturing Members may have $5M-$50M revenue range. SBA 504 has specific revenue thresholds; confirm the specific tier.
- **Quantify expansion scope in dollars with SBA 504 structure visibility.** Specialty manufacturing Members evaluating SBA 504 need to see the full acquisition cost broken into bank first lien (50%), CDC second lien (40%), and borrower equity (10%). Capture as Sized magnitude with structural breakdown noted.
- **Build the financing-scenario model with SBA 504 + alternatives comparison.** Specialty manufacturing Members evaluate SBA 504 against conventional CRE alternatives (TRACK-003) where owner-occupancy permits both. Model 2-3 structures so the CFO/controller can engage with the structural choice.

### 1.3 Consult — Specialty Manufacturing (replaces v2 cell)

What to focus on during the Consult phase with a specialty manufacturing Member:

- **Show the SBA 504 structure clearly.** Specialty manufacturing Members evaluate the 50/40/10 structure (bank first / CDC second / borrower equity) more readily than they evaluate single-structure recommendations. Surface the three-piece architecture so the structural advantages (longer term on the CDC piece, fixed rate exposure, lower equity requirement than conventional CRE) become visible.
- **Listen for board-process and timing language.** "We need to bring this to the board at our next meeting" surfaces specific timeline. Capture as Indecision with co_decision_maker_input tag plus timeline reference.
- **Capture multiple Reactions across the conversation.** Specialty manufacturing Members react differently to operational fit (banker shows structural understanding) vs financial fit (rate, terms, covenants on bank piece) vs structural fit (SBA 504 mechanics, CDC partner integration) vs relationship fit (specialist coordination, decision-timeline match). Capture Reaction state on each dimension.
- **Surface specialist coordination explicitly.** SBA 504 requires CRE specialist + SBA specialist + CDC partner coordination. Surface the coordination structure during Consult, not as Navigate-phase surprise. The CDC partner involvement is structurally distinct from conventional CRE; bankers should explain it explicitly rather than assuming Member understands.
- **Confirm relationship-led positioning when appropriate.** Specialty manufacturing Members with long Blaze relationships often prefer structuring with Blaze when the case is competitive. The "we'd like the next round to be with you" framing matters; capture Member's stated preference.

### 1.4 Navigate — Specialty Manufacturing (replaces v2 cell)

What to focus on during the Navigate phase with a specialty manufacturing Member:

- **Initiate SBA 504 specialist handoff with full context package.** SBA specialist receives the captured evidence package (Track context, captured Signals, financing scenarios, board-process timeline, owner-occupancy confirmation). Surface the handoff as scheduled working session with all parties (CRE specialist + SBA specialist + CDC partner contact + Member's CFO/controller), not isolated email introduction.
- **Coordinate board-presentation timeline including SBA 504 structure explanation.** Specialty manufacturing Members often need a banker-supported board presentation. SBA 504-specific content (CDC partner, structural advantages, longer-term commitment to property) needs to be in the board materials. Offer this coordination explicitly; capture as ActionCard with specific timeline.
- **Track external-advisor-review milestones.** External advisors (legal, financial, CPA) typically review SBA 504 structure before commitment. The CDC partner's documentation requirements add review touchpoints. Track each review milestone via ActionCards.
- **Confirm post-commitment relationship structure.** Specialty manufacturing Members typically continue treasury, working capital, and other relationship deepening post-SBA-504-commitment. Surface the post-commitment relationship structure during Navigate.
- **Maintain specialist-handoff oversight with awareness of SBA 504 timing.** SBA 504 financings often take 90-150 days from Navigate to closing (longer than conventional CRE due to CDC documentation). Banker maintains relationship oversight while specialists drive structure execution; capture progression milestones.

## Section 4 — Editorial review prompts for Francisco

Five places where banker's-eye and product-voice judgment matter:

**E1 — SBA 504 structural specificity.** The 50/40/10 structure references appear throughout specialty_manufacturing sections. Worth verifying this lands as appropriately educational without becoming over-mechanical. Most uncertain: Section 1.3 Consult bullet about "Show the SBA 504 structure clearly" — the structural detail might overwhelm banker who hasn't internalized SBA 504 mechanics.

**E2 — Owner-occupancy framing.** The owner-occupancy distinction is structurally critical for SBA 504 vs conventional CRE choice. Worth verifying language around it lands as factual rather than exclusionary. Section 1.1 Discover bullet emphasizes capturing this early; verify timing feels right.

**E3 — Cross-Track upsell vs natural reference.** Sections 2 and 3 minor additions reference adjacent Tracks (Vehicle, Equipment, Investment Property, PACE, Business Visa). The line between "natural cross-product awareness" and "upsell discipline overreach" matters. Worth checking each bullet feels consultative rather than sales-flavored.

**E4 — HVAC PACE-as-customer-tool framing.** Section 3.2 surfaces PACE as financing tool for HVAC's customers (relationship-deepening even when HVAC Member isn't the borrower). This is a legitimate consultative move but may read as advisory overreach for some bankers. Worth deciding whether to keep, soften, or drop.

**E5 — Specialty manufacturing Coach density.** Section 1's four cells are dense (5-6 bullets each). v2 had similar density but for different content. With SBA 504-specific content layered on, the surface may feel substantial. Worth checking visual review whether to trim from 5-6 bullets to 4 for visual breathing room.
